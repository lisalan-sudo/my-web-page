My name is Lisa Lan, and I've spent 17 years immersed in the landscapes, peoples and rhythms of Southwest China. I grew up fascinated by the Miao and Dong minority cultures — their embroidery, their silver, their songs passed down through generations without a written word.

What I offer isn't a packaged tour. It's a genuine connection — to the land, the people, and a way of travel that feels more like visiting a friend's homeland than ticking boxes off a list. Whether you want total luxury or a humble homestay in a Miao village, I make it extraordinary.

Common Questions

Everything You Need
to Know Before You Go

Do I need a Chinese visa to visit Guizhou?
Citizens of 54+ countries can now enter China visa-free for up to 30 days (as of 2024). I can advise you on the current requirements for your specific country and help you plan your entry point. Guiyang has an international airport with connections via major Asian hubs.
What is the best time of year to visit Guizhou?
Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer the best weather and landscape. However, the real answer depends on what you want to see — I can match your dates to the perfect festival or seasonal experience. Winter is underrated for authentic village life.
Do you cater for solo travellers and couples as well as groups?
Absolutely. Most of my most memorable trips have been with solo travellers or couples who wanted a deeply personal experience. I design every journey from scratch regardless of group size — from 1 person to small groups of up to 8.
Is Guizhou safe for international travellers?
Guizhou is one of the safest provinces in China for international visitors. The local people are famously warm and welcoming. Having a local guide like me means you also have someone who knows every area intimately and can ensure you're comfortable at every step.
Can you arrange the full trip including flights and accommodation?
Yes. I offer full trip planning including domestic flights, high-speed rail, private transfers, accommodation at all levels (from boutique hotels to authentic homestays), daily guiding, and restaurant reservations. You can arrive with nothing planned — I handle everything.
Can we combine Guizhou with other provinces?
This is one of my specialities. I cover all of Southwest China — Guizhou, Yunnan, Guangxi, Sichuan and Hunan. A 10–21 day Southwest Grand Tour combining multiple provinces is one of the most rewarding journeys I offer. Just tell me what calls to you.
